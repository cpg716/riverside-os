# Pilot receiving and inventory guide

**Audience:** Receiving staff, inventory leads, managers.

**Purpose:** Live pilot procedure for receiving, interrupted drafts, cost variance, inventory adjustments, and physical inventory safety.

---

## Receiving basics

If a physical inventory session is open or in review, receiving is paused. Sales may continue during physical inventory, but do not post received stock until the count is published or canceled.

1. Open the vendor paperwork from Receive Stock.
2. Confirm vendor and PO/invoice.
3. Scan or enter received quantities.
4. Review cost changes.
5. Enter invoice number when available.
6. Post receipt only when quantities and costs match the paperwork.

**Draft means inventory is not updated yet. Posted means inventory and accounting trail are updated.**

---

## Interrupted receiving

If interrupted:

1. Stop at a clean point.
2. Leave the draft visible if returning soon.
3. Tell the next receiver what has been counted.
4. Do not post from memory.
5. If the receiver leaves, manager reviews the draft before end of day.

Before resuming:

1. Confirm vendor paperwork.
2. Confirm what was already scanned.
3. Continue or intentionally stop.

---

## Cost variance

If cost looks different:

1. Pause before posting.
2. Compare to vendor paperwork.
3. Confirm freight/extra charges.
4. Ask manager if variance is unexpected.
5. Add notes if the reason matters.

Do not ignore cost warnings just to clear receiving.

---

## Inventory adjustments

During pilot, adjustments are manager-reviewed.

Required note must explain the real reason:

- Count correction.
- Damaged item.
- Lost/missing item.
- Receiving correction.
- Return to vendor.
- Customer-service recovery.

Do not use vague notes.

---

## Physical inventory safety

Physical inventory should not happen during rush periods.

Before starting:

1. Assign area and owner.
2. Confirm scanners work.
3. Confirm staff understand counted vs reviewed vs published.
4. Confirm receiving is paused until the count is published or canceled. Sales may continue.

Before publishing:

1. Review missing items.
2. Review large shrink/surplus.
3. Confirm receiving stayed paused and sales activity was considered.
4. Manager approves publish.
5. Do not publish if the review is stale or confusing.

---

## End-of-day inventory review

Manager reviews:

- Open receiving drafts.
- Posted receipts.
- Cost warnings.
- Inventory adjustments.
- Physical inventory sessions open, reviewing, published, or canceled.
- Any negative or surprising availability called out by staff.
