# Pilot incident severity guide

**Audience:** All pilot staff.

**Purpose:** Decide how serious an issue is and what action is required.

---

## Severity levels

### Informational

Use when something is worth noting but did not slow the store or create risk.

Examples:

- Staff asked where a button is once.
- A label felt unclear but work completed correctly.
- A non-urgent report took longer than expected.

Required action:

1. Log if repeated or useful.
2. Review at end of day only if pattern appears.

---

### Watch closely

Use when the same friction could become a training or speed issue.

Examples:

- Two staff ask the same workflow question.
- A cashier hesitates before parking or resuming a sale.
- Wedding member status takes too long to explain.
- Alteration schedule next step is missed once.

Required action:

1. Log the issue.
2. Manager reviews during daily pilot review.
3. Coach before next shift if repeated.

---

### Operational concern

Use when store work slows, a manager must intervene, or the next step is unclear.

Examples:

- Customer waits while staff determine whether a refund completed.
- Register close has a blocker staff cannot identify.
- Receiving draft is left unfinished without owner.
- RMS account selection or QBO staging requires same-day review.

Required action:

1. Manager owns the issue.
2. Record customer, transaction, amount, and next action.
3. Resolve or assign before close.

---

### Pilot blocker

Use when a workflow cannot be trusted without supervision.

Examples:

- Checkout, refund, or close cannot be completed reliably.
- Staff cannot tell whether a wedding pickup is safe.
- Repeated RMS or QBO confusion creates accounting review risk.
- Inventory adjustment or receiving post cannot be explained.

Required action:

1. Stop unsupervised use of the affected workflow.
2. Manager or owner decides fallback.
3. Pilot lead reviews before next shift.

---

### Rollback-triggering incident

Use when the pilot may need to stop or revert.

Examples:

- Payment state is unclear and cannot be reconciled.
- Register close cannot be trusted.
- Refund or RMS charge creates unresolved financial risk.
- QBO or inventory posting cannot be verified.
- Multiple customer-facing failures occur in the same day.

Required action:

1. Halt the affected workflow.
2. Manager notifies owner immediately.
3. Preserve receipts, screenshots, and notes.
4. Decide continue, limit, or rollback before more live use.
