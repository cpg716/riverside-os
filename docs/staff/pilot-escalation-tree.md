# Pilot escalation tree

**Audience:** All pilot staff.

**Purpose:** Make escalation ownership obvious during live store pressure.

---

## First rule

If money, garment release, inventory posting, RMS, QBO, or register close is uncertain, pause the workflow and call the right owner. Do not guess.

---

## Escalation path

| Starting point | Escalate to | When |
|----------------|-------------|------|
| Cashier | Manager on duty | Payment uncertainty, refund, blocked checkout, parked sale confusion, close warning. |
| Wedding consultant | Wedding lead, then manager | Wrong member risk, balance due, pickup uncertainty, missing measurements, appointment conflict. |
| Tailor/alterations staff | Alterations lead, then manager | Rush due date conflict, ticket missing, ready-for-pickup confusion, unscheduled intake. |
| Receiving/inventory staff | Inventory lead, then manager | Draft/post uncertainty, cost variance, damaged item, adjustment, physical count issue. |
| Manager | Accounting owner | Refund review, QBO warning, RMS reconciliation, deposit/balance concern. |
| Manager | Technical owner | Hardware failure, repeated blocked workflow, system unavailable, repeated recovery issue. |
| Manager/accounting/technical | Owner | Pilot blocker, rollback-triggering incident, customer-facing financial risk. |

---

## When to pause workflow

Pause immediately when:

- Payment result is unclear.
- Refund amount or tender is unclear.
- Register close shows unresolved blockers.
- Customer/member identity is uncertain.
- Garment pickup has balance or status confusion.
- Inventory or receiving post cannot be explained.
- Staff are about to repeat a payment, refund, close, QBO post, or stock adjustment.

---

## When to stop using a feature

Manager may stop a feature for the day when:

- The same issue happens twice in one shift.
- Staff cannot recover without help.
- The workflow creates customer waiting during rush.
- The result affects payment, accounting, inventory, or garment release.
- Staff cannot explain what happened next.

---

## When to use fallback

Use fallback only with manager approval when:

- The customer cannot wait.
- A required hardware device is down.
- The system is unavailable.
- A workflow is blocked but store work must continue.

Fallback must include customer/member, amount, item, time, staff, and manager owner.

---

## When to halt pilot operations

Halt the pilot or affected workflow when:

- A payment, refund, RMS charge, QBO post, or close result cannot be reconciled.
- More than one customer-facing financial incident occurs in a day.
- Staff cannot close safely.
- The store cannot recover interrupted work.
- Owner decides rollback is safer than continuing.
