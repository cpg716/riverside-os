# Pilot governance rules

**Audience:** All live pilot staff.

**Purpose:** Rules that keep the Riverside OS pilot controlled, auditable, and recoverable.

---

## Manager-only during pilot

These require manager or assigned lead control:

- Register close.
- Refunds/exchanges after payment.
- Manager Access approvals.
- Card/payment uncertainty.
- Cash discrepancy review.
- Releasing wedding garments with balance due or unclear member identity.
- Inventory adjustments.
- Physical inventory publish.
- QBO approval/posting.
- RMS Charge exceptions and reconciliation.
- Backup/restore or destructive settings.

---

## Manager-reviewed daily

Manager reviews daily:

- Z-report and tender totals.
- Cash over/short notes.
- Refunds/exchanges.
- Offline recovery.
- Parked sales.
- Wedding pickups and balance exceptions.
- Alteration unresolved work.
- RMS Charge follow-up.
- QBO staging.
- Inventory adjustments.
- Receiving drafts/posts.
- Physical inventory sessions.

---

## Prohibited behaviors

Do not:

- Share Access PINs.
- Retry uncertain card payments blindly.
- Clear browser storage to fix checkout.
- Force register close around blockers.
- Release unpaid garments without approval.
- Use vague inventory adjustment notes.
- Post QBO rows with unresolved warnings just to finish the day.
- Mark RMS Charge reported before external follow-up is complete.
- Start physical inventory during customer rush.
- Promise stock, pickup, or alteration readiness without checking the record.

---

## Fallback rules

When Riverside OS is unavailable or uncertain:

1. Stop the workflow.
2. Preserve customer, amount, item, and time details.
3. Use the store-approved paper/manual fallback only with manager approval.
4. Keep payment evidence.
5. Enter or reconcile later under manager ownership.
6. Do not hide fallback work from daily reconciliation.

---

## Documentation expectations

Every exception needs:

- Customer/member when applicable.
- Transaction or receipt when applicable.
- Amount when money is involved.
- Register/staff/date/time.
- Plain reason.
- Manager owner.
- Next action.

---

## Daily pilot review expectations

At end of day, pilot lead reviews:

- What slowed staff down.
- What customers waited on.
- What required manager override.
- What was interrupted and resumed.
- What remained unresolved.
- What needs training before the next shift.

Repeat issues should become next-day coaching or SOP updates.
