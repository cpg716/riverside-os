## Opening

1. Sign in to Back Office.
2. Confirm each register station is online and using the correct Register number.
3. Open Register #1 first when using a shared till group.
4. Test receipt printer, scanner, card terminal, and cash drawer before the first customer.
5. Check Operations Dashboard for tasks, pickups, warnings, and messages.
6. Check wedding pickups due today and any members with balance due or missing measurements.
7. Check alterations due today, overdue work, rush work, and ready-for-pickup work.
8. Confirm there are no blocked offline recovery items before normal selling starts.

**Opening rule:** Do not begin live pilot sales until register, printer, scanner, card terminal, and recovery status are reviewed.

---

## Rush-hour priorities

1. Keep checkout moving.
2. Park interrupted sales instead of leaving uncertain carts open.
3. Call a manager early for refunds, card uncertainty, balance disputes, or pickup confusion.
4. Do not do physical inventory, large inventory adjustments, or QBO posting during customer rush.
5. For wedding pickup, verify member, balance, garment readiness, and partial pickup notes before release.
6. For alterations, finish the customer-facing intake first, then schedule or hand off before switching tasks.

---

## Blocked workflow escalation

| If this is blocked | First owner | What to do now | Manager action |
|--------------------|-------------|----------------|----------------|
| Checkout | Cashier | Stop retrying after one safe retry; park if possible | Review payment/recovery state |
| Card payment | Cashier | Do not rerun blindly | Confirm terminal/provider outcome |
| Refund/exchange | Cashier | Pause before tendering | Verify original transaction and return path |
| Wedding pickup | Consultant | Do not release garment | Verify member, balance, and notes |
| Alteration handoff | Tailor/cashier | Keep customer context visible | Assign schedule, ticket, or queue owner |
| Receiving | Receiver | Do not post from memory | Review quantities, invoice, cost variance |
| Register close | Authorized closing staff | Use the ordinary close after required inputs | Repair warnings when practical; otherwise document the owner and verify **Unresolved Issues at Close** |
| QBO staging | Accounting | Do not post warning-bearing rows casually | Review warnings and approve only when ready |

---

## Recovery ownership

- Cashier owns the customer conversation until a manager accepts the issue.
- Manager owns register close, payment uncertainty, refund exceptions, and release exceptions.
- Receiving owner owns open receiving drafts until posted or intentionally stopped.
- Accounting owns QBO posting and daily financial signoff.
- Store owner or pilot lead owns unresolved issues that repeat more than once in a day.

---

## Close responsibilities

1. Finish or park active sales intentionally.
2. Review offline recovery. Repair it when practical; otherwise assign an owner. The ordinary authorized close remains available and must preserve the exact warning under **Unresolved Issues at Close**.
3. Review card/payment issues before final close.
4. Count cash and enter close notes for over/short beyond store tolerance.
5. Print or save the Z-report.
6. Review refunds/exchanges from the shift.
7. Review parked sales, RMS Charge follow-ups, pending wedding pickups, and unresolved alterations.
8. Hand accounting the QBO/RMS/refund/deposit checklist.

---

## Accounting handoff

Accounting or manager reviews:

- Z-report and tender totals.
- Refunds/exchanges and refund tender evidence.
- Deposits, balances due, and wedding pickup releases.
- RMS Charge sales/payments and R2S reporting status.
- QBO staged journals, warnings, approved-not-posted rows, and failed rows.
- Inventory adjustments, receiving posts, and physical inventory sessions.
