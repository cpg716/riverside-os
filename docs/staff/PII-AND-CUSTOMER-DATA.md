# Customer data and PII (staff)

**Audience:** All staff who use CRM, POS, weddings, or appointments.

This page is **not** legal advice. Use it for **consistent, cautious** handling inside Riverside OS. Your employer’s **written policy** wins when it is stricter.

---

## What counts as sensitive

- **Name, phone, email, address** on customer profiles.
- **Measurements** and **fit** notes.
- **Wedding party** relationships, dates, and financial roles.
- **Order history** and **payment** context visible in hub screens.

---

## Do

- Verify **identity** before reading balances, addresses, or alteration details aloud.
- Use **customer_code** or internal search when coaching a coworker instead of broadcasting full phone numbers on the floor.
- Log **manager** involvement for **merge**, **delete**, or **export** requests — [customers-back-office.md](customers-back-office.md).
- Follow **marketing opt-in** flags before discussing SMS/email campaigns — see product behavior in **Customers** and messaging docs.

---

## Do not

- Paste **full customer records**, **PINs**, **API keys**, or **backup** paths into **chat apps**, **SMS to personal phones**, or **public screenshots**.
- **Merge** duplicate customers or **delete** profiles without **manager** alignment.
- Use **personal devices** to photograph screens with customer data unless **policy** explicitly allows.

---

## AI and “ask the bot” tools

- If the store deploys **in-app help** indexed on `docs/staff`, do not **paste** live customer data into third-party AI chat windows.
- Prefer **hypothetical** examples (“customer A, order B”) when testing.

---

## If you suspect a breach or misuse

- Stop **sharing** further; notify **manager** or **owner** per [STORE-SOP-TEMPLATE.md](STORE-SOP-TEMPLATE.md) escalation list.
