# Pilot manager operations guide

**Audience:** Managers, leads, store owner.

**Purpose:** Manager control guide for Riverside OS live pilot operations.

---

## Manager role during pilot

The manager is responsible for:

- Approving exceptions.
- Owning blocked workflows.
- Keeping staff from retrying uncertain payments.
- Reviewing register close warnings and their archived evidence.
- Reviewing refunds, RMS Charge, QBO, inventory adjustments, and unresolved work daily.
- Recording repeat confusion for pilot follow-up.

---

## Overrides

Use Manager Access only when you understand the reason.

Before approving:

1. Ask what staff tried.
2. Confirm customer, transaction, amount, and reason.
3. Confirm the action is allowed by store policy.
4. Approve only once the next step is clear.

Do not approve:

- A refund when the original transaction is uncertain.
- A completed-sale void unless you have confirmed customer, receipt, amount, tender, reason, and the next refund/reversal step.
- A payment retry when provider outcome is unknown.
- A wedding release when balance or member identity is uncertain.
- QBO posting with unresolved warnings.
- Inventory changes without a real reason note.

---

## Close warnings

If close shows a warning:

1. Identify whether it is a nonblocking checkout/payment recovery warning or a true required input: cash count, check review, Daily Cash Deposit date, or an over-$5 discrepancy note.
2. Assign one owner.
3. Resolve recovery warnings before final close whenever practical.
4. If unresolved at close, document why, who owns it, and next review time.

Use the ordinary authorized close after required inputs are complete. Never dismiss or hide a warning to make the day look clean; verify it appears under **Unresolved Issues at Close**, where later repair cannot rewrite the archived evidence.

---

## Reconciliation

Daily manager review must include:

- Z-report.
- Cash over/short notes.
- Check numbers.
- Card/payment uncertainty.
- Refunds and exchanges.
- Completed-sale voids and their refund/reversal status.
- Parked sales.
- Offline recovery.
- RMS Charge sales/payments.
- Open deposits and balances due.
- Wedding pickups.
- Receiving drafts/posts.
- Inventory adjustments.
- Physical inventory sessions.
- QBO staging status.

Use [pilot-daily-reconciliation-checklist.md](pilot-daily-reconciliation-checklist.md).

---

## Offline recovery

When recovery is pending or blocked:

1. Stop staff from retrying the same sale.
2. Confirm customer, time, amount, register, and payment method.
3. Review the recovery queue.
4. Resolve before register close when possible.
5. If it cannot be resolved, document owner and next action.

Never recreate an unknown completed sale. Assign it for recovery and verify it appears under **Unresolved Issues at Close**; ordinary authorized close remains available and does not resolve the sale.

---

## RMS Charge review

Daily:

1. Review RMS Charge sales and payments.
2. Confirm each POS-created RMS Charge item has R2S follow-up assigned.
3. Confirm payment collections are cash/check only when required by store policy.
4. Mark reported only after the external follow-up is actually complete.
5. Review exceptions and mismatches.

---

## QBO review

Only accounting or an assigned manager should approve/post during pilot.

Before approving:

1. Confirm business date.
2. Review blocking warnings.
3. Review refund, deposit, RMS, gift card, store credit, tax, and merchant clearing warnings.
4. Confirm balanced does not mean automatically safe.
5. Post only when ready for accounting handoff.

Failed QBO rows are follow-up work, not cashier work.

---

## Inventory adjustments

Manager reviews all adjustment notes daily.

Allowed reasons include:

- Count correction.
- Damage/loss.
- Receiving correction.
- Return to vendor.
- Customer-service recovery.

Do not allow vague notes like "fixed", "test", or "inventory".

---

## Pilot observation duties

Watch for:

- Staff hesitating at refund/exchange steps.
- Staff using void when a return, exchange, or payment review is the correct workflow.
- Repeated manager override requests.
- Repeated checkout recovery or payment uncertainty.
- Wedding pickup confusion.
- Alteration intake saved but not scheduled.
- Receiving drafts left open.
- QBO warnings repeating.
- Register close stress.

Record repeat issues for end-of-day pilot review.
