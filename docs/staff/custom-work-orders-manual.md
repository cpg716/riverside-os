# Staff Manual: Custom Work Orders & Rush Tracking

This guide covers how to handle **Custom Work Orders** (MTM items like suits, shirts, and slacks) and how to mark orders as **Rush** in the POS.

## 1. When to use a Custom Work Order
Use this flow when a customer is ordering an item that is **not** in our standard floor catalog (e.g., a Made-To-Measure suit where the price and details are determined during the consultation).

## 2. Booking a Custom Item in POS
1.  **Add the Item**: Scan or type a SKU starting with `CUSTOM` (e.g., `CUSTOM-SUIT`, `CUSTOM-SHIRT`).
2.  **Configure the Order**: A popup will automatically appear. You must fill out the following:
    *   **Item Type**: Choose from SUITS, SPORT COAT, SLACKS, or INDIVIDUALIZED SHIRTS.
    *   **Sale Price**: Enter the agreed-upon price for this item.
    *   **Need By Date**: Enter the date the customer requires the item.
    *   **Rush Order**: Toggle this **ON** if the order is urgent or expedited.
    *   **Gift Wrap**: Toggle this if the customer requested presentation packaging.
3.  **Confirm**: Tap **Confirm** to add the configured line to the cart.

## 3. Rush Orders & Deadlines
Marking an order as **Rush** or setting a **Need By Date** does the following:
*   **Dashboard Priority**: The order will appear at the top of the "Suggested next" queue on the Register Dashboard for all staff.
*   **Urgency Indicator**: A "Rush" badge and a countdown (e.g., "Need by 4/15") will appear in order lists.
*   **Red Alert**: Items due within 48-72 hours will show red status indicators to signify extreme urgency.

## 4. Fulfillment
Custom items arrive without a preset cost. When the physical item is received from the vendor:
1.  Procurement will link the vendor cost to the order line.
2.  The customer is notified for pickup.
3.  Check out the customer normally to fulfill the order.
