# Pilot wedding operations guide

**Audience:** Wedding consultants, managers, pickup staff.

**Purpose:** Live pilot procedure for wedding member verification, pickup, balance review, appointments, and escalation.

---

## Before helping a wedding customer

1. Search the customer.
2. Confirm the wedding party name.
3. Confirm event date.
4. Confirm the member name and role.
5. Confirm whether the customer is the member, payer, or another party contact.

**Rule:** Do not use only first name or family name for pickup decisions.

---

## Member verification

Before editing or releasing anything:

1. Read the member name back.
2. Confirm party/event date.
3. Check measurements status.
4. Check appointment status.
5. Check balance due.
6. Check garment readiness.
7. Check notes and recent history.

If any item is unclear, stop and get a manager or wedding lead.

---

## Pickup flow

1. Open the party/member.
2. Confirm member identity.
3. Confirm balance is paid or manager-approved.
4. Confirm garment pieces are ready.
5. Confirm alteration status if applicable.
6. For full pickup, confirm all pieces are leaving.
7. For partial pickup, write exactly what left and what remains.
8. Save pickup only after the customer-facing facts are verified.

**No unpaid garment release during pilot without manager approval.**

---

## Balance review

If balance is due:

1. Tell customer there is a balance to resolve before release.
2. Do not hand over garments.
3. Open the correct payment path or call manager.
4. Confirm payment posts to the correct member/transaction.
5. Return to pickup only after balance is clear.

---

## Partial pickup

Partial pickup requires a note.

Good notes:

- "Jacket and vest picked up. Pants remain for hem."
- "Member picked up shirt only. Suit remains pending final fitting."
- "Bride picked up all groomsmen ties; jackets remain in store."

Bad notes:

- "Partial."
- "Done."
- "Customer took some."

---

## Appointment status

Before promising readiness:

1. Check next appointment.
2. Check missed/canceled appointments.
3. Confirm measurements are complete.
4. Confirm fitting status if needed.
5. Confirm tailor notes.

If appointment status and garment status disagree, escalate.

---

## Escalation rules

Call manager/wedding lead for:

- Wrong member selected or suspected.
- Balance due but customer says paid.
- Pickup requested before garment is ready.
- Partial pickup without clear remaining pieces.
- Missing measurements near event date.
- Appointment conflict or reassignment.
- Customer dispute about who paid or who can pick up.
