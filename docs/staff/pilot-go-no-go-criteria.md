# Pilot go/no-go criteria

**Audience:** Owner, pilot lead, manager on duty, accounting lead.

**Purpose:** Decide whether to continue, limit, pause, or roll back the pilot.

---

## Daily status colors

| Status | Meaning | Decision |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Green | Pilot is operating safely with normal observation. | Continue. |
| Yellow | Pilot is usable but needs manager observation or training. | Continue with controls. |
| Red | Pilot has unresolved operational, financial, or customer-facing risk. | Limit, pause, or roll back affected workflow. |

---

## Acceptable thresholds

Continue as Green when:

- Register close reconciles.
- Refunds and exchanges are explainable and reviewed.
- No unresolved payment state remains at close.
- QBO/RMS issues are assigned and not blocking reconciliation.
- Staff can complete core workflows without repeated coaching.
- Customer waits are normal for retail service.

---

## Yellow thresholds

Continue with governance when:

- Same staff question repeats during the day.
- One workflow needs manager observation.
- One customer-facing delay occurs but is resolved.
- One recovery flow is used and reviewed successfully.
- QBO/RMS/receiving work is held for review but clearly assigned.
- Register close completes with documented notes.

Required action:

1. Record in daily review.
2. Assign owner.
3. Coach before next shift.

---

## Red thresholds

Limit or pause a workflow when:

- Two similar operational concerns happen in one shift.
- A staff member cannot recover interrupted work.
- Customer/member identity or garment pickup status is uncertain.
- Refund or payment confidence depends on memory instead of record evidence.
- Register close requires unresolved manual interpretation.
- Inventory or receiving result is unclear after posting.

Required action:

1. Stop unsupervised use of the affected workflow.
2. Assign manager owner.
3. Decide before next shift whether to continue, limit, or roll back.

---

## Rollback thresholds

Start rollback decision review when:

- Payment, refund, RMS, QBO, inventory, or close cannot be reconciled.
- A customer-facing financial error cannot be resolved same day.
- Register close cannot be trusted.
- Staff must repeatedly bypass or avoid a critical workflow.
- Multiple workflows produce unresolved issues in one day.
- Owner decides store confidence is lower than fallback confidence.

---

## Required reconciliation accuracy

Before continuing to the next pilot day:

- Cash expected vs actual must be reviewed.
- Card/payment uncertainty must be resolved or assigned.
- Refunds must match tender evidence.
- RMS charge activity must be reviewed.
- QBO staging must be reviewed or intentionally held.
- Open balances, deposits, and wedding pickups must be reviewed.
- Inventory posts and adjustments must have clear notes.
