# Pilot alterations operations guide

**Audience:** Cashiers, tailors, alterations desk, managers.

**Purpose:** Live pilot procedure for alteration intake, scheduling, ticket handoff, rush handling, ready-for-pickup, and unresolved work.

---

## Intake

1. Attach the customer first.
2. Confirm the garment source:
   - Current cart item.
   - Past purchase.
   - SKU lookup.
   - Manual/custom item.
3. Enter work requested in plain words.
4. Enter due date.
5. Mark rush only when manager/tailor approves rush handling.
6. Add notes the tailor can act on.
7. Save the intake.

**Rule:** Intake is not complete until the next step is assigned.

---

## After intake: choose the next step

Before moving to another customer, decide:

- Schedule now.
- Send to tailor queue.
- Print ticket.
- Add to current sale/checkout.
- Assign manager/tailor follow-up.

If no next step is chosen, the work is unresolved.

---

## Scheduling

1. Check customer availability.
2. Check tailor capacity.
3. Confirm due date.
4. Avoid impossible rush promises.
5. Save appointment/schedule details.
6. Tell customer the next date/time clearly.

If the customer cannot schedule now, assign follow-up before ending the interaction.

---

## Ticket handoff

Ticket/notes should answer:

- Customer name.
- Garment.
- Work requested.
- Due date.
- Rush or normal.
- Pickup promise.
- Special notes.

Tailor should not need to ask the cashier what the ticket means.

---

## Rush handling

Rush requires:

1. Tailor or manager review.
2. Due date confirmed.
3. Capacity accepted.
4. Customer expectation stated.
5. Clear notes.

Do not mark rush just because the customer is upset.

---

## Ready-for-pickup

Before marking ready:

1. Confirm work is complete.
2. Confirm garment is present.
3. Confirm balance or payment status if applicable.
4. Send/trigger customer notification only when ready status is true.
5. Keep pickup notes clear.

---

## Unresolved work

Unresolved alteration work includes:

- Intake saved but not scheduled.
- Ticket missing.
- Rush not approved.
- Due date conflict.
- Customer notified but garment not ready.
- Ready garment with balance confusion.

Manager/tailor lead reviews unresolved work daily.
