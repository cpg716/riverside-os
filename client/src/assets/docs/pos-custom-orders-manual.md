# Custom Orders (MTM) & Rush Fulfillment

## Role: Salesperson
### Purpose: Managing bespoke items and expedited delivery timelines.

Custom Work Orders are used for Made-To-Measure (MTM) items where price, composition, and delivery dates vary per transaction.

---

## 1. Booking a Custom Item

1. **Add the Item**: Scan or type a SKU beginning with `CUSTOM` (e.g., `CUSTOM-SUIT`, `CUSTOM-SHIRT`).
2. **The Configuration Modal**: A setup screen will automatically trigger. You MUST provide:
   - **Type**: Select the garment category (Suits, Slacks, etc.).
   - **Sale Price**: Enter the consultation-quoted price.
   - **Required Date**: Use the **Need By Date** calendar to set the fulfillment deadline.
   - **Rush Toggle**: Enable this if the order is expedited and requires priority tracking.
3. **Save**: Tap **Confirm** to lock the configuration into the cart.

---

## 2. Rush Tracking & Priority

Marking an item as **Rush** or setting an aggressive **Need By Date** triggers system-wide priority:
- **Action Board Visibility**: Rush orders jump to the top of the **Priority Feed** for all staff.
- **Visual Alert**: A "Rush" badge and timeline countdown (e.g., "Due in 3 days") will appear in the Orders Workspace.
- **Red Alert**: Items due within **72 hours** will display red status icons to signal immediate logistical action.

---

## 3. Fulfillment Lifecycle

1. **Booking**: The line item is added to the cart and the transaction is finalized.
2. **Receiving**: When the custom garment arrives from the vendor, Procurement links the actual cost to the transaction line.
3. **Pickup**: The customer is notified. Use the **Orders** recall to process the final pickup and recognize revenue.

---

## Troubleshooting

- **Configuration Pop-up Didn't Appear**: Ensure the SKU used starts with the `CUSTOM` prefix.
- **Wrong Price Entered**: Tap the item in the cart and select **Re-configure** to update the sale amount before checkout.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Always verify the **Need By Date** with the customer before finalizing. This date is used for all logistics and shipping estimates.
